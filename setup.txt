The random setup in both implementations follows a structured, multi-phase process designed to ensure a balanced and geometrically interesting starting position. It centers around three "anchor" pieces (the Goddess and two Heroes) placed on opposite edges of the board, with other pieces clustered around them in distance-based "rings."

### 1. The Placement Sequence & Conditions

The setup occurs in the following order for each side (White on top, Black on bottom):

#### Phase A: Anchor Placement (Goddess & Heroes)
*   **Target Area**: Polygons on the "physical edges" (Top for White, Bottom for Black).
*   **Conditions**: 
    *   **Hero Separation**: The two Heroes must be far apart (distance > 6 steps).
    *   **Goddess Guard**: The Goddess must be "guarded" but not "crowded" by her Heroes. Both Heroes must be between 3 and 6 steps away from her.
*   **Process**: The engine randomly picks three edge polygons and checks these conditions. If they fail, it shuffles and tries again until a valid "anchor triangle" is formed.

#### Phase B: Protectors (Berserkers)
*   **Target Area**: Immediately adjacent to the Goddess (Distance 1).
*   **Condition**: If the Goddess is too close to the edge and has fewer than 2 neighbors, the range expands to Distance 2.
*   **Result**: 2 Berserkers are placed in these "inner ring" slots to act as immediate bodyguards.

#### Phase C: The "Ring" System (Sets 1-4)
*   The engine generates four concentric sets of polygons based on their distance from the three anchors (Goddess + 2 Heroes):
    *   **Set 1**: Distance 1 from any anchor.
    *   **Set 2**: Distance 2 from any anchor (excluding Set 1).
    *   **Set 3**: Distance 3 from any anchor (excluding Sets 1 & 2).
    *   **Set 4**: Distance 4 from any anchor (excluding Sets 1-3).
*   These sets are shuffled individually and then flattened into a single "priority list" of available polygons (`set_all`).

#### Phase D: Chromatic Spread (Bishops)
*   **Condition**: Bishops must be spread across different colored polygons to maximize their potential (since they move only on their starting color).
*   **Process**: The engine looks at all unique colors available in the `set_all` list, shuffles them, and assigns one unique color to each of the 4 Bishops.

#### Phase E: Infantry Filling (Ghouls & Sirens)
*   The remaining pieces (9 Ghouls and 9 Sirens) are added to the queue. 
*   They are placed in the remaining available slots in `set_all`, meaning they naturally fill the closest available rings first.

#### Phase F: The Reserves (Mages & Soldiers)
*   **Constraint**: Mages (1) and Soldiers (9) are **never placed on the board** during setup. 
*   They remain in the "returned" (Stock) state, requiring players to deploy them onto the board during their turns (usually via a Mage or on a matching color).

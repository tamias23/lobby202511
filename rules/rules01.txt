# Game Rules (Rust Implementation) — rules01.txt

Comprehensive rules derived exclusively from the Rust codebase
(engine.rs, models.rs, rules_tests.rs).

## Game Objective

Capture the opponent's **Goddess**. The function `perform_random_turn` returns
`true` when a Goddess is captured, signalling a game-ending event.

## Players & Sides

Two players: **White** and **Black**. White always takes the first turn.

## The Board

The board is a tessellation of coloured polygons. Each polygon has:
- A **colour** (e.g. grey, yellow, blue, orange).
- Two distinct adjacency lists:
  - **slide neighbours** (`neighbors`): used for Soldier/Ghoul chain movement,
    Bishop/Mage AoE blasts, Siren pin checks, and deployment adjacency.
  - **jump neighbours** (`neighbours`): used for leap-based movement ranges
    (Goddess, Heroe, Mage, Bishop, Siren) and setup distance calculations.

## Pieces & Quantities (per player)

| Piece      | Count |
|------------|:-----:|
| Goddess    |   1   |
| Heroe      |   2   |
| Bishop     |   4   |
| Mage       |   1   |
| Soldier    |   9   |
| Ghoul      |   9   |
| Siren      |   9   |
| Berserker  |   2   |
| **Total**  | **37**|

## Piece Statuses

A piece is in exactly one of three states:
- **On the board** — positioned on a polygon.
- **Returned** — off the board, eligible for re-deployment.
- **Graveyard** — permanently dead (no legal moves).
  Note: in the current Rust implementation, captured pieces are set to
  `"returned"`, so they can always be re-deployed.

---

## 1. Setup Phase (`setup_random_board`)

The board starts empty. Pieces are placed procedurally for each side:

1. **Goddess & Heroes (Edge Anchoring)**
   - White uses **top-edge** polygons; Black uses **bottom-edge** polygons.
   - An edge polygon is one where `polygon.points.len() > neighbour_count`
     and the centre is within board margins.
   - The Goddess is placed on a random edge polygon.
   - Each Heroe is placed on a different edge polygon such that:
     - The two Heroes are **more than 6 jump-hops apart** from each other.
     - Each Heroe is **between 4 and 6 jump-hops** from the Goddess
       (not within 3, but within 6).

2. **Berserkers (Protector Placement)**
   - Placed within **1 jump-hop** of the Goddess (expanded to 2 if needed).

3. **Bishops (Chromatic Spread)**
   - Placed so each Bishop sits on a polygon of a **different colour**.
   - Candidate polygons are drawn from sets 1–4 hops away from anchors.

4. **Ghouls & Sirens (Infantry)**
   - Filled into remaining unoccupied polygons within 1–4 hops of anchors.

5. **Mage & Soldiers (Reserve)**
   - Remain in the **"returned"** state (off-board) at game start.

## 2. The Turn Sequence (`perform_random_turn`)

### 2.1 Colour Choice Phase

At the start of a turn (`is_new_turn == true`):

1. The active player evaluates every polygon colour on the board.
2. A colour is **valid** only if choosing it would grant at least one legal
   move for at least one friendly piece (including deployments of "returned"
   pieces).
3. One valid colour is chosen. If **no** colour yields any legal move, the
   turn is skipped entirely and play passes to the opponent.

### 2.2 Piece Activation

After choosing a colour, a piece is **eligible to act** if:
- It is in the **"returned"** state, **OR**
- It is on a polygon whose colour matches the **chosen colour**, **OR**
- It is the **sequence-locked piece** (see §2.3).

### 2.3 Sequence Locking & Turn-End Rules (`apply_move_turnover`)

After every move, the engine checks the colour of the **destination polygon**
against the **chosen colour**:

#### A. Piece lands on the CHOSEN COLOUR

| Piece type             | Condition                      | Result                                 |
|------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Deployed from reserve  | (any type)                     | **Turn ends immediately.**             |
| Heroe                  | captured something, counter==0 | Heroe gets **one extra move** (locked). |
| Soldier / Berserker    | (always)                       | Piece is **sequence-locked**; only it may move next. It may also choose "PASS_TURN" to voluntarily end. |
| Any other piece        | (always)                       | **Turn ends immediately.**             |

#### B. Piece lands on a DIFFERENT colour

| Piece type             | Condition                      | Result                                 |
|------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Heroe                  | captured something, counter==0, not from reserve | Heroe gets **one extra move** (locked). |
| Any piece              | (otherwise)                    | Sequence lock is **cleared**. The turn does **NOT** end — the player may pick another eligible piece from the chosen colour. |

**Key rule:** landing on a non-chosen colour never ends the turn (unless the
player has no remaining legal moves).

### 2.4 Turn Transition

When a turn ends:
- `turn_counter` increments.
- Active side switches to opponent.
- Chosen-colour selections are cleared.
- `locked_sequence_piece` is reset.
- `heroe_take_counter` is reset to 0.

## 3. Movement Rules (`get_legal_moves`)

Movement range is computed via **breadth-first flood-fill** on the jump
adjacency graph, stopping at `max_dist` hops.

### 3.0 Global Constraints

- A piece can **never** move onto a polygon occupied by a **friendly** piece.
- A piece can **never** move onto or capture a **Berserker** (any side).
- **Siren** and **Bishop** can **never capture**; they may only move to
  **empty** polygons.
- If a piece is **sequence-locked** to another piece, it has **zero legal
  moves** (only the locked piece may act).

### 3.1 Siren Pin

Before computing any moves, the engine checks slide-neighbours of the piece's
current polygon. If any slide-neighbour is occupied by an **enemy Siren**, the
piece is **pinned** and has **no legal moves** at all.

### 3.2 Goddess

- **Range:** 2 jump-hops.
- **Constraint:** standard (no landing on friendlies or Berserkers).

### 3.3 Heroe

- **Range:** 3 jump-hops.
- **Take limit:** if `heroe_take_counter >= 2`, the Heroe has **zero** legal
  moves (it has already used its bonus move).

### 3.4 Mage

- **Range:** 3 jump-hops.
- **Colour constraint:** can only land on polygons of a **different colour**
  than the polygon it starts on.

### 3.5 Bishop

- **Range:** 4 jump-hops.
- **Colour constraint:** can only land on polygons of the **same colour** as
  the polygon it starts on.
- **Cannot capture** (filtered out — empty polygons only).

### 3.6 Siren

- **Range:** 2 jump-hops.
- **Cannot capture** (filtered out — empty polygons only).
- **Passive pin aura:** any enemy piece slide-adjacent to a Siren is pinned
  (see §3.1).

### 3.7 Soldier & Berserker

Movement is computed via **chain traversal** on the **slide** adjacency graph:

1. Starting from the piece's polygon, explore slide-neighbours.
2. A neighbour can be **traversed through** (added to the chain) if:
   - It is occupied by a **friendly piece**, **OR**
   - It is **empty**, its colour matches the **chosen colour**, and it is
     **not slide-adjacent to an enemy Siren** (Siren pin on intermediate
     empty polygons).
3. From every polygon in the chain (including the origin), all
   slide-neighbours become candidate destination targets.
4. Standard filters apply (no friendlies, no Berserkers on the target).

This "phalanx" mechanic allows Soldiers and Berserkers to traverse vast
distances via friendly-piece chains and chosen-colour highways.

### 3.8 Ghoul

Movement uses a **depth-limited chain** on the **slide** adjacency graph:

1. From the current polygon, all slide-neighbours are valid targets (depth 0).
2. If `depth < 2`, the Ghoul can **chain through** a slide-neighbour if:
   - The polygon is **empty**, **AND**
   - Its colour is **not** the chosen colour, **AND**
   - It is **not slide-adjacent to an enemy Siren**.
3. From every chained polygon, slide-neighbours are again added as targets.
4. Maximum chain depth is **2** (so the Ghoul can reach up to 3 slide-hops
   away via chaining).

## 4. Capture & Area-of-Effect Abilities (`apply_move`)

### 4.1 Standard Capture

When a piece moves to an occupied polygon, the defender is set to
**"returned"** (eligible for re-deployment). The attacker occupies the polygon.

### 4.2 Bishop AoE (Landing Blast)

After a Bishop moves (**whether or not it captured** — though Bishops cannot
capture, so this always fires on empty landings):
- All enemy pieces on **slide-adjacent** polygons are destroyed (set to
  "returned").
- **Berserkers are immune** to this blast.

### 4.3 Mage AoE (Chain Attack)

After a Mage moves, **only if** the Mage captured a piece on the target
polygon:
- All enemy pieces on **slide-adjacent** polygons of the landing site are
  destroyed (set to "returned").
- **Berserkers are immune** to this blast.

If the Mage lands on an empty polygon, no AoE triggers.

## 5. Deployment / Re-entering the Board

A piece in the **"returned"** state can be placed back on the board. Legal
deployment targets are empty polygons satisfying **at least one** of:

1. **Chosen-colour match:** the polygon's colour matches the active player's
   chosen colour for this turn.
2. **Mage adjacency:** the polygon is **slide-adjacent** to a friendly Mage
   that is currently on the board.

### 5.1 Bishop Deployment Restriction

If the piece being deployed is a **Bishop**, an additional constraint applies:
the target polygon must **not** be slide-adjacent to any **enemy** piece. This
prevents fragile support pieces from deploying into contested zones. The
**Mage** does *not* have this restriction and may be deployed adjacent to
enemy pieces.

### 5.2 Deployment & Turn-End

Deploying a piece onto the chosen colour **immediately ends the turn** (see
§2.3-A). Deploying onto a non-chosen colour does not end the turn.

---

## 6. Berserker Invulnerability

The Berserker is a special defensive piece:
- **Cannot be targeted:** no piece may move onto or capture a Berserker.
- **Immune to AoE:** Bishop blasts and Mage chain attacks skip Berserkers.
- **Can be traversed by friendlies:** friendly Soldiers/Berserkers can chain
  through a friendly Berserker just like any other friendly piece.
- **Blocks enemy chaining:** enemy Soldiers/Berserkers cannot chain through
  a Berserker (same as any enemy piece — only friendly pieces are traversable).
- **Moves like a Soldier:** same chain/phalanx movement mechanics.

## 7. Summary of Topology Usage

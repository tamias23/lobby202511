# Game Rules (Rust Core Implementation) — rules02.txt

This document captures the definitive game rules as implemented in the `./new_main/rust-core` engine. These rules are the source of truth for the modern platform.

## Game Objective

The objective is to capture the opponent's **Goddess**. A game ends immediately when a Goddess is removed from the board (captured or destroyed by an AoE effect).

## Players & Sides

- **White** vs. **Black** (internal representation may use "Yellow" for Black).
- White always takes the first turn of the game.

## The Board

The board consists of a tessellation of coloured polygons. Each polygon has two distinct adjacency graphs:
1. **Jump Adjacency (`neighbours`)**: Used for leap-based movement (Goddess, Heroe, Mage, Witch, Siren) and setup distance calculations.
2. **Slide Adjacency (`neighbors`)**: Used for chaining movement (Soldier, Minotaur, Ghoul), AoE effects (Witch, Mage), Siren pin checks, and deployment proximity.

## Pieces & Quantities (per player)

| Piece      | Count | Movement Type | Base Range |
|------------|:-----:|---------------|------------|
| Goddess    |   1   | Jump          | 2 hops     |
| Heroe      |   2   | Jump          | 3 hops     |

| Mage       |   1   | Jump          | 3 hops     |

## 1. Setup Phase (`setup_random_board`)

Pieces are placed in stages with specific spatial constraints:

1. **Goddess & Heroes (Edge Anchors)**
   - Goddess is placed on a random edge polygon (top-edge for White, bottom-edge for Black).
   - Heroes are placed on the same edge, but must be **more than 6 jump-hops apart** from each other.
   - Each Heroe must be **between 4 and 6 jump-hops** from their own Goddess (not within 3, but within 6).

2. **Minotaurs (Guardians)**
   - Placed within **1 jump-hop** of the Goddess (expanded to 2 if no space is available).

3. **Witchs (Chromatic Spread)**
   - Placed on polygons of **different colours** from one another.
   - Candidates are usually within 1–4 hops of existing anchors.

4. **Ghouls & Sirens (Infantry)**
   - Filled into unoccupied polygons within 1–4 hops of anchors.

5. **Mage & Soldiers (Reserve)**
   - Start the game in the **"returned"** state (off-board).

## 2. Turn Sequence

### 2.1 The New Turn (`get_legal_colors`)
1. At the start of a turn, the active player **must choose a colour**.
2. A colour is valid if it allows at least one piece to make a legal move (on-board or deployment).
3. If no colour allows a move, the turn is skipped.

### 2.2 Piece Eligibility
A piece can move if:
- It is in the **"returned"** state (is being deployed).
- Its current polygon colour matches the **chosen colour**.
- It is currently **sequence-locked** (e.g., a Soldier/Minotaur that landed on chosen color or a Heroe after a capture).

### 2.3 Turnover Logic (`apply_move_turnover`)
Landing on a polygon triggers checks that determine if the move or turn ends:

#### A. Deployed from Reserve
- If the piece lands on the **chosen colour**, the **turn ends immediately**.
- If it lands via Mage adjacency on a **different colour**, the **turn does NOT end** (Rule 79).

#### B. Pinned by Siren (Rule 108)
- If a piece lands slide-adjacent to an enemy Siren, its move ends immediately.
- The **turn ends** ONLY if the piece is pinned on a **chosen colour** polygon.

#### C. Landing on Chosen Colour (Rule 15/16)
- **Soldiers & Minotaurs**: The piece becomes **sequence-locked**. The turn does NOT end; this piece must move again (or pass) before other pieces can act.
- **Other Pieces**: The **turn ends immediately**.

#### D. Landing on Different Colour (Rule 14)
- Sequence locks are cleared. The **turn does NOT end**. The player may continue acting with other eligible pieces (on-colour pieces).
- **Heroe Exception**: If a Heroe captures a piece and lands on a different colour (and hasn't used its bonus move yet), it becomes **sequence-locked** for one extra move (Rule 44).

## 3. Movement Rules

### 3.1 Global Restrictions
- No piece may land on a polygon occupied by a **Friendly** piece.
- No piece may land on or capture a **Minotaur** (Ally or Enemy).
- **Siren** and **Witch** cannot capture; they only move to empty polygons.
- **Siren Pin**: A piece slide-adjacent to an enemy Siren has **zero legal moves** (immobilized).

### 3.2 Specific Piece Ranges
- **Goddess**: 2 jump-hops.
- **Heroe**: 3 jump-hops. Limited to one bonus move per turn after a capture.
- **Mage**: 3 jump-hops. **Must** land on a colour different from its start.
- **Witch**: 4 jump-hops. **Must** land on a colour matching its start.
- **Siren**: 2 jump-hops. Passive pin aura (slide topology).
- **Soldier / Minotaur (Phalanx)**: May traverse a chain of slide-adjacent polygons. A chain can pass through friendly pieces or empty chosen-colour polygons (if not pinned by a Siren). The piece can then "step/capture" to any slide-adjacent polygon from any point in the chain.
- **Ghoul**: Depth-limited chain (max 2 internal hops). Can chain through empty, non-chosen-colour polygons (if not pinned). Reaches up to 3 slide-hops total.

---

## 4. Captures & Area-of-Effect (AoE)

### 4.1 Captures
Moving onto an enemy-occupied polygon (non-Minotaur) returns that piece to the **"returned"** stash.

### 4.2 Initial AoE Blasts (`apply_move`)
- **Witch (Landing Blast)**: After moving to an empty polygon, all enemy pieces slide-adjacent to the destination are destroyed.
- **Mage (Chain Attack)**: After **successfully capturing** an enemy piece, all enemy pieces slide-adjacent to the destination are destroyed.
- **Minotaurs are immune** to all AoE blasts.

---

## 5. Deployment Rules

Pieces in the "returned" stash can re-enter the board on empty polygons if:
1. The polygon matches the **chosen colour**.
2. The polygon is **slide-adjacent to a friendly Mage** already on the board.

**Witch Restriction**: A Witch cannot be deployed slide-adjacent to **any enemy piece**.

---

## 6. Winning & Termination
The game ends immediately if:
- A Goddess is captured.
- A player has no valid moves and no legal colours to choose (Stalemate/Loss).

## 7. Numerical Rule References (Code Documentation)

The following rule numbers are referenced in the engine source code (`engine.rs`) for logic branching and implementation safety:

### **Rule 11: Color Choice Dependency**
- **Description**: No piece eligibility until color selection.
- **Explanation**: A turn cannot start and no piece is authorized to move until the active player selects a valid board color.

### **Rule 14: Non-Termination Color**
- **Description**: Landing on a different color does NOT end the turn.
- **Explanation**: Moving to a polygon whose color differs from the chosen color ends only that piece's move. The turn remains with the active player, allowing other pieces on the chosen color to act.

### **Rule 15: Move Termination**
- **Description**: Landing on the chosen color ends the MOVE.
- **Explanation**: For most pieces, landing on a chosen-color polygon completes its action. This typically triggers a turn end unless a sequence lock is active.

### **Rule 16 / Rule 74: Piece-Specific Sequence Locking**
- **Description**: Soldier and Minotaur "lock the sequence" instead of ending the turn.
- **Explanation**: When these pieces land on a chosen-color polygon and if they are not immobilized by a siren, they become "sequence locked." Only this piece may move again during the current turn until the sequence is broken by landing on a different color or passing.

### **Rule 44: Heroe Bonus Action**
- **Description**: Heroe bonus move after capture.
- **Explanation**: A Heroe that captures an enemy piece is granted one immediate bonus move, if the landing color is not the turn-chosen-color (provided it hasn't already used its bonus move this turn) and if it is not immobilized by a siren.

### **Rule 78 / Rule 79: Deployment Turn-End Variations**
- **Description**: **Rule 78**: Entering on chosen color ends turn. **Rule 79**: Entering via Mage on different color does NOT end turn.
- **Explanation**: Standard deployments to the chosen color yield the turn. High-skill deployments via Mage adjacency to non-chosen colors allow the player to continue their turn.

### **Rule 108: Siren Pin Termination**
- **Description**: Immobilization ends the MOVE.
- **Explanation**: Moving into a position slide-adjacent to an enemy Siren results in immediate immobilization (pinning). This ends the move instantly. If the pin occurs on a chosen-color polygon, the entire turn ends.

### **Rule 110: Chromatic Unlock (Mage Gate)**
- **Description**: The Mage is locked until all 4 board colors have been chosen.
- **Explanation**: At the start of the playing phase, the Mage piece cannot be deployed or acted upon. A global, persistent set (`colors_ever_chosen`) tracks every distinct board color that has been selected as the turn color by either player since the beginning of the playing phase. Once this set contains all 4 distinct board colors (`grey`, `green`, `blue`, `orange`) — regardless of which player chose them — the Mage becomes permanently available to both players simultaneously (Chromatic Unlock). The UI displays a progress counter showing how many colors have been seen, which disappears on unlock.

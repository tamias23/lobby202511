# Game Rules: Lobby 202511

here are comprehensive rules for a two-player polygon-based board game.

## General Information
- **Players:** 2 players (White vs. Black).
- **The Board:** The game is played on a board composed of connected, colored polygons (e.g., grey, yellow, blue, orange). 
- **Game Objective:** The primary objective is to capture the opponent's **Goddess**. (Note: While not yet hard-coded into the `endOfTurn` loop, this is the traditional win condition given the Goddess's unique protected setup).

## The Core Turn Sequence
The turn-based loop is completely driven by the board's terrain colors. Players alternate turns following this sequence:
  1. **Choose a Color:** At the start of their turn, a player must choose a terrain color from the selector UI (e.g., grey, yellow, blue, orange).
  2. **Activate Pieces:** The player can **only** pick up and move pieces that are currently standing on polygons matching that exact chosen color.
  3. **Continuous Actions:** Moving a piece to a polygon of a *different* (unchosen) color does NOT end the turn. The player can continue moving other valid pieces from the chosen color, effectively doing as much as they can.
  4. **Ending the Turn:** The turn **immediately ends** if any piece completes a movement and lands exactly on a polygon of the *chosen color*. 
     *Exception:* Because Soldier and Berserker have "chain/phalanx" movement, if they land on the chosen color, they do not end the turn immediately; instead, they lock the active sequence so the player can *only* continue moving that specific piece until they finish their path or manually end the turn. If a Soldier or Berserker is "captured" by an opposin siren on the chosen color, then the turn ends (as the piece is indeed ending its movement on the tchosen color)

## The Pieces & Quantities
Each player commands an army of 37 pieces:
- **1 Goddess**
- **2 Heroe**
- **4 Bishop**
- **1 Mage**
- **9 Soldier**
- **9 Ghoul**
- **9 Siren**
- **2 Berserker**

## 1. Setup Phase
The game begins with an empty board and pieces are placed sequentially:
1. **Goddess & Heroe:** The White player uses the top edge polygons, and the Black player uses the bottom edge polygons. The Goddess is placed on a random edge polygon. The two Heroe must be placed exactly 2 polygons away from the Goddess (and not adjacent to each other).
2. **Berserker:** Must be placed exactly 1 polygon away from the Goddess.
3. **Bishop:** Must be placed such that each of the four Bishop starts on a polygon of a different color.
4. **Other Pieces:** Soldier, Ghoul, Siren, and the Mage are populated onto the remaining empty polygons close to the Heroe and Goddess (spreading out 1 to 4 polygons away), following specific color-matching constraints.

## 2. Movement & Capture Rules
Pieces capture enemy pieces by moving onto an occupied polygon. Each piece has unique movement algorithms and ranges:

### The Goddess
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **2 polygons** away.
  
### The Heroe
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **3 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** A Heroe is able to jump one additional time if he captures an enemy piece. If he takes, he can play again, exactly once.

### The Bishop
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **4 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** A Bishop can only land on polygons that are the **same color** as the polygon it started its move on. It is color-bound.
- **Area of Effect:** When a Bishop moves to an empty polygon, it emits an attack that destroys all adjacent enemy pieces (except Berserker).

### The Mage
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **3 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** Opposite to the Bishop, a Mage **cannot** land on a polygon of the same color as the one it started on. 
- **Area of Effect (Chain Attack):** When the Mage lands directly on an occupied polygon to capture a piece, it destroys both the target piece AND any adjacent pieces belonging to the SAME TEAM (White or Black) as the target (except Berserker).

### The Siren
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **2 polygons** away.
- **Passive Aura:** Her most powerful attribute is an aura effect. Any enemy piece that stands on a polygon adjacent to a Siren is **pinned** (its `canMove` flag is set to 0), stripping it of its ability to move until the Siren vacates the area or is captured.

### The Ghoul
- **Movement:** Can move **1 polygon** away to capture or move to an empty space.
- **Chain Jumping:** If the adjacent polygon is empty, has no enemy Siren bordering it, and is **not** the player's "chosen color" for the turn, the Ghoul can chain jumps up to **3 polygons** away in a single move.

### The Soldier
- **Movement:** Can move **1 polygon** away.
- **Phalanx Movement:** Soldier can chain their movements by passing through :
  1. Contiguous polygons occupied by **friendly pieces**.
  2. Empty polygons that match the player's **"chosen color"** for the turn (provided those specific polygons are not adjacent to an enemy Siren).
, allowing them to travel vast distances across the board.
- **Restriction:** They cannot pass through enemy Berserker or paths blocked by enemy Siren.

### The Berserker
- **Movement:** Behaves similarly to the Soldier (they can also chain their moves through friendly pieces and empty polygons of the chosen color).
- **Invulnerability:** Acts as a heavy barricade. All pieces are strictly forbidden from moving into or capturing them. Furthermore, Berserkers are entirely **invulnerable to Area of Effect attacks**—they cannot be destroyed by a Mage's chain attack or a Bishop's adjacent blast.

## 3. Supplementary Mechanics
- **Entering / Re-entering the Board:** A piece that is currently off the board (destroyed or unused) can be placed back onto the board during a player's turn. There are exactly two ways a piece can enter:
  1. It can be placed on any **empty polygon** that matches the player's **"chosen color"** for the turn. (This immediately ends the turn).
  2. It can be placed on any empty polygon adjacent to a **friendly Mage**. (This does not end the turn unless it is placed on the chosen color).
  *Special Exception:* A **Bishop** or **Mage** is restricted when entering play. It cannot be placed on a polygon that is adjacent to an enemy piece, regardless of which of the two placement methods is used.
- **Color Selection:** The first decision in a turn is the choice of a terrain color. All valid piece pickups and the `endOfTurn` triggers revolve entirely around this selected color.

# Game Rules: Lobby 202511

Based on the game's JavaScript codebase, here are the comprehensive rules for this two-player polygon-based board game.

## General Information
- **Players:** 2 players (White vs. Yellow).
- **The Board:** The game is played on a board composed of connected, colored polygons (e.g., white, yellow, black, orange). 
- **The Core Turn System:** The turn-based loop is completely driven by the board's terrain colors.
  1. At the start of their turn, a player must **choose a color**.
  2. The player can **only** pick up and move pieces that are currently standing on polygons matching that exact chosen color.
  3. Moving a piece (or multiple pieces) to polygons of a *different* unchosen color does NOT end the turn. The player can continue moving other valid pieces.
  4. The turn **immediately ends** if any piece completes a movement by landing exactly on a polygon of the chosen color (with an exception for Soldiers/Trifoxes, which lock the player into continuing to move only that piece until they manually click End Turn or land elsewhere).

## The Pieces & Quantities
Each player commands an army of 37 pieces:
- **1 Goddess**
- **2 Kings**
- **4 Bishops**
- **1 Mage**
- **9 Soldiers**
- **9 Ghouls**
- **9 Sirens**
- **2 Trifoxes**

## 1. Setup Phase
The game begins with an empty board and pieces are placed sequentially:
1. **Goddess & Kings:** The White player uses the top edge polygons, and the Yellow player uses the bottom edge polygons. The Goddess is placed on a random edge polygon. The two Kings must be placed exactly 2 polygons away from the Goddess (and not adjacent to each other).
2. **Trifoxes:** Must be placed exactly 1 polygon away from the Goddess.
3. **Bishops:** Must be placed such that each of the four Bishops starts on a polygon of a different color.
4. **Other Pieces:** Soldiers, Ghouls, Sirens, and the Mage are populated onto the remaining empty polygons close to the Kings and Goddess (spreading out 1 to 4 polygons away), following specific color-matching constraints.

## 2. Movement & Capture Rules
Pieces capture enemy pieces by moving onto an occupied polygon. Each piece has unique movement algorithms and ranges:

### The Goddess
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **2 polygons** away.
  
### The King
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **3 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** A King is able to jump one additional time if he captures an enemy piece. If he takes, he can play again, exactly once.

### The Bishop
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **4 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** A Bishop can only land on polygons that are the **same color** as the polygon it started its move on. It is color-bound.
- **Area of Effect:** When a Bishop moves to an empty polygon, it emits an attack that destroys all adjacent enemy pieces (except Trifoxes).

### The Mage
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **3 polygons** away.
- **Special Limitation:** Opposite to the Bishop, a Mage **cannot** land on a polygon of the same color as the one it started on. 
- **Area of Effect (Chain Attack):** When the Mage lands directly on an occupied polygon to capture a piece, it destroys both the target piece AND any adjacent pieces belonging to the SAME TEAM (White or Yellow) as the target (except Trifoxes).

### The Siren
- **Movement:** Can leap up to **2 polygons** away.
- **Passive Aura:** Her most powerful attribute is an aura effect. Any enemy piece that stands on a polygon adjacent to a Siren is **pinned** (its `canMove` flag is set to 0), stripping it of its ability to move until the Siren vacates the area or is captured.

### The Ghoul
- **Movement:** Can move **1 polygon** away to capture or move to an empty space.
- **Chain Jumping:** If the adjacent polygon is empty, has no enemy Sirens bordering it, and is **not** the player's "chosen color" for the turn, the Ghoul can chain jumps up to **3 polygons** away in a single move.

### The Soldier
- **Movement:** Can move **1 polygon** away.
- **Phalanx Movement:** Soldiers can chain their movements by passing through :
  1. Contiguous polygons occupied by **friendly pieces**.
  2. Empty polygons that match the player's **"chosen color"** for the turn (provided those specific polygons are not adjacent to an enemy Siren).
, allowing them to travel vast distances across the board.
- **Restriction:** They cannot pass through enemy Trifoxes or paths blocked by enemy Sirens.

### The Trifoxes
- **Movement:** Behaves similarly to the Soldier (they can also chain their moves through friendly pieces and empty polygons of the chosen color).
- **Blocking & Invulnerability:** Acts as a heavy barricade. Enemy Soldiers and Trifoxes are strictly forbidden from moving into or capturing them. Furthermore, Trifoxes are entirely **invulnerable to Area of Effect attacks**—they cannot be destroyed by a Mage's chain attack or a Bishop's adjacent blast.

## 3. Supplementary Mechanics
- **Entering / Re-entering the Board:** A piece that is currently off the board (destroyed or unused) can be placed back onto the board during a player's turn. There are exactly two ways a piece can enter:
  1. It can be placed on any **empty polygon** that matches the player's **"chosen color"** for the turn. (This immediately ends the turn).
  2. It can be placed on any empty polygon adjacent to a **friendly Mage**. (This does not end the turn unless it is placed on the chosen color).
  *Special Exception:* A **Bishop** or **Mage** is restricted when entering play. It cannot be placed on a polygon that is adjacent to an enemy piece, regardless of which of the two placement methods is used.
- **Color Selection:** The first decision in a turn is the choice of a terrain color. All valid piece pickups and the `endOfTurn` triggers revolve entirely around this selected color.
